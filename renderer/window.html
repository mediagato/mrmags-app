How to talk to me.

Six phrases that get the most out of Mr. Mags. Say them naturally — Claude does the rest.

  1. "Remember that I <fact about you or your work>." Files it away. Comes back next chat.
  2. "What do you know about my <students / clients / project>?" Recalls everything tagged to that topic.
  3. "Use my usual rubric / template / format." Pulls a saved pattern, reuses it verbatim.
  4. "Save this as my <name> pattern." Stores the current output as a reusable template.
  5. "Forget what I said about <topic>." Removes that memory. Done.
  6. "What's in my brain?" Lists everything I'm holding onto.

None of this leaves your machine. Your memory file lives at ~/Library/Application Support/Mr. Mags/brain.

    About

    Named for the teacher who inspired it.

    Mr. Mags is named for a school teacher whose students call him Mr. Mags. He was the first user. We built it for him because his lesson plans, rubrics, and parent emails kept evaporating into one-shot AI chats that forgot him the moment he opened a new conversation.

    Now Claude remembers. Every conversation carries the context of every previous one.

    Everything you tell Mr. Mags lives on your machine. There is no cloud, no account, no server-side database with your name on it. The promise isn't "we won't read your data" — it's that we can't, because we don't have it.
